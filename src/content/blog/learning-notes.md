# Learning Notes

After completing several courses and side projects, I've noticed a pattern in what actually sticks vs. what fades.

## What works

**Building something small immediately** — even a toy project forces you to hit real errors and look up real solutions. Passive watching rarely creates durable knowledge.

**Teaching it** — writing a blog post about what you learned (like this one) forces you to find gaps in your own understanding.

**Spaced repetition** — revisiting material after a few days, then a week, then a month.

## What doesn't work (for me)

- Watching a tutorial at 2x speed and feeling productive
- Taking beautiful notes I never review
- Trying to understand everything before building anything

The best course isn't the one with the highest rating — it's the one you actually finish and apply.
